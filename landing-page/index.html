Why it matters

Nature is a financial risk.
Most companies aren't measuring it.

More than half of global GDP depends on functioning ecosystems — clean water, stable soil, healthy fisheries, climate regulation. When biodiversity declines, the economic systems built on top of it become fragile. This is no longer a conservation issue. It is a material business risk.

Two types of risk

Your company is exposed
in both directions.

Biodiversity risk is not one-dimensional. TNFD distinguishes between what your operations do to nature, and what nature does to your operations. BioESG scores both — separately, so you can act on each.

Impact risk
What your operations do to nature
Mining, plantations, and large-scale construction in biodiverse regions directly destroy or fragment habitat. As disclosure requirements tighten under GRI 304 and TNFD, untracked impact becomes a regulatory and reputational liability.
Dependency risk
What nature does for your operations
Agriculture depends on pollinators. Fisheries depend on reef health. Manufacturers depend on clean water. If the ecosystem your operations rely on degrades, so does your supply chain — quietly, until it isn't.
Biodiversity health
The baseline that determines both
A region with high species diversity is both more sensitive to disturbance and more ecologically resilient. Understanding it is the precondition for assessing either risk accurately — and for meeting location-specific GRI 304 disclosure requirements.
Regulatory trajectory
The policy window is narrowing
TNFD launched in 2023. GRI 304 was revised and expanded. The EU mandates nature reporting for 50,000 companies. Indonesia's biodiversity is globally significant — and globally watched. Voluntary disclosure is becoming mandatory faster than most expect.

How it works

From a province name
to a disclosure-ready report.

No consultants required for the initial assessment. Three steps, three scores.

01
Input your operating context
Select your province, industry category, operation size in hectares, and answer five dependency questions. Takes under three minutes.
02
We process real ecological data
Species occurrence records from GBIF are converted into Shannon diversity and species richness scores — standardized ecological indices used in peer-reviewed research.
03
Receive three actionable scores
Biodiversity health, impact risk, and dependency risk — each with GRI 304 disclosure references and TNFD exposure classification built in.
